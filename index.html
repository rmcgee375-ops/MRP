Material Requirements Planning (MRP)

A practical guide to how manufacturers plan materials, avoid shortages, and keep production running smoothly.

What is MRP?

Material Requirements Planning (MRP) is a system used in manufacturing to determine:

  • What materials are needed
  • How much is needed
  • When the materials are needed

The goal is simple: ensure materials arrive just in time for production‚Äîwithout excess inventory or costly shortages.

The Three Core Inputs

  • Master Production Schedule (MPS) ‚Äì what finished products will be made and when
  • Bill of Materials (BOM) ‚Äì the complete list of components required for each product
  • Inventory Records ‚Äì what is on hand, on order, and supplier lead times

How MRP Works

  1. Start with product demand from the MPS
  2. Explode the BOM into component requirements
  3. Subtract existing inventory and scheduled receipts
  4. Offset requirements by supplier lead times
  5. Generate planned purchase and work orders
Example:
To build 100 bicycles, MRP calculates required wheels, frames, and parts, subtracts inventory, and schedules orders so everything arrives before assembly begins.
